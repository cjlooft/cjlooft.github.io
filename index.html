DYNAMIC STRETCHING

Dynamic stretches are done before exercise to prepare your muscles for movement.
You stretch your muscles while moving around.

Why?
Some benefits of consistent stretching:
• Better flexibility, mobility and range of motion -ease of movement- bend, reach and turn further

• Less aches, pains and stiff, tight muscles and joints

• Less recovery time and muscle soreness

• Reduced susceptibility to soft tissue injuries - muscle and tendon strains

BASIC ROUTINE


Stand tall with your feet roughly shoulder-width apart, your core engaged, and your posture tall and straight.
* New

DYNAMIC HUG (deltoid 'shoulder' stretch)
(Use controlled motions)
1.Reach arms out and wide.
2. Movement - bring your arms across your chest,
hugging yourself -right arm on top of your left
3. swing your arms out wide again...
4. than swing your arms back into a hug position,
left arm on top of your right.

Recommended for at least 30 seconds.
